The Tower of Babel

The Tower of Babel by Pieter Bruegel the Elder (1563)

One of the most wondrous myths in the history of mankind, the Tower of Babel represents the might of humans who were so united and resilient as to build the pride of their civilization so tall that it reached heaven.

Scholars have tried to link the Tower of Babel to known structures. One such example is the Etemenanki, a ziggurat dedicated to the Mesopotamian god Marduk in Babylon.
